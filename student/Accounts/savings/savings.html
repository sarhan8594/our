Savings

A savings account is a type of bank account that is designed to help individuals save money and earn interest on their deposits. Here are some full details about savings accounts
  • Purpose: A savings account is meant for the purpose of depositing and saving money. It is not intended for regular transactions like paying bills or making purchases.
  • Interest rate: Savings accounts earn interest on the money deposited. The interest rate varies depending on the bank and the type of account. The interest is usually calculated on a daily or monthly basis and paid out annually or monthly.
  • Minimum balance: Most banks require a minimum balance to be maintained in a savings account to avoid penalties or fees. The minimum balance requirement varies between banks and account types.
  • Withdrawal limits: There are limits on the number of withdrawals that can be made from a savings account in a given period. The number of withdrawals allowed and the penalty for exceeding the limit also varies between banks and account types.
  • Access: Savings accounts can be accessed through online banking, mobile banking, ATM, or by visiting a bank branch.
  • FDIC insurance: Savings accounts are typically insured by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) in the United States, which means that the account holder's deposits are insured up to a certain amount in case of bank failure.
